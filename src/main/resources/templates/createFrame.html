Tips

Hi, here are some tips on arguments and critical issues.
The argument scheme can help you create an argument quickly and efficiently. If you are not familiar with this, you can click on the "Example of Argument Scheme" below to learn how to create an argument using the template. Similarly, you can click on the "Example of Critical Question" below to see from which perspectives other users will challenge your argument.
You can select the argument option you are interested in by clicking on the button in the top left hand corner of the page. Once you have selected your argument option, the example of argument scheme and critical questions on the right will change.

Example of Argument Scheme

Action Scheme

In the current circumstances R:

 COVID-19 is spreading rapidly.

We should perform action A:

 Full lockdown.

Which will result in new circumstances S:

 The number of confirmed cases is falling.

Which will realise goal G:

 Stop the spread of COVID-19.

Which will promote value V:

 Public health.

Expert Opinion Scheme

E is an expert in domain D. E asserts that A is known to be true. A is within D. Therefore, A may (plausibly) be taken to be true.

Major Premise:

 Doctor Tom is an expert in opthalmology, a field that knows the human eye.

Minor Premise:

 Doctor Tom says that looking at mobile phone with the lights off for long periods at night can lead to eye damage.

Conclusion:

 Looking at a mobile phone with the lights off at night for a long time can lead to eye damage.

Popular Opinion Scheme

Generally Acceptance Premiese:

It is generally accepted by people in Hawaii  that the sea is a great place to swim in the summer.

Presumption Premise：

This is not compulsory to fill in here.

Conclusion:

The sea around Hawaii seems to be a good place to swim in the summer.

Position to Know Scheme

Major Premise:

 Guide Tom is a local Londoner who knows the locations of London's attractions, so he should know where the British Museum is.

Minor Premise:

 Guide Tom said the British Museum is located in Great Russell Street.

Conclusion:

 It is true that the British Museum is in Great Russell Street.

Analogy Scheme

Similarity Premise:

 Tigers and whales both are viviparous.

Base Premise:

 Tigers are mammals.

Conclusion:

  Whales are mammals.

Correlation to Cause Scheme

Correlation Premise:

 In 1986 at a conference in Boston on the link between humans and pets, researchers reported that pets could lower blood pressure in humans, improve heart patients’ chances of survival, and could even penetrate the isolation of autistic children.

Conclusion:

 Owning a pet can improve health.
The example from: Walton, Douglas. Fundamentals of critical argumentation (Cambridge University Press, 2005), pp. 102.
